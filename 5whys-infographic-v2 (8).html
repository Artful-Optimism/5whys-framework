Strategic Application — 3 Things It Forces You To See
→
Systems, Not People
Blame shuts down learning. Reframe every question away from individuals and toward structure. "Why did John miss the deadline?"
→ "Why do deadlines get missed in our org?"
◈
Hidden Assumptions
Every "why" cracks open something the team assumed was fixed, funded, or solved. When someone says "we can't do X — budget constraints," keep asking. The real block is rarely money. It's how budget decisions get made — and that's fixable.
⇄
Competing Priorities
Most strategic problems aren't failures — they're tensions. Two objectives pulling against each other, never openly acknowledged. The 5 Whys surfaces those conflicts so leadership can actually deal with them instead of patching symptoms forever.
The Power Move — Add This After Every Why
"What would have to be true for this problem not to exist?"
This reframes the conversation from what broke to what has to change. Done right, it doesn't just fix a problem — it forces teams to question how the whole system operates. That's where sustainable competitive advantage comes from. Not the quick fix. The real one.
